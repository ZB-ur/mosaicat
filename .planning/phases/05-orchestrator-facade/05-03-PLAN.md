**IMPORTANT — avoid these pitfalls (from research):**
1. Do NOT change the constructor signature or public method signatures
2. Both run() and resumeRun() must funnel through the SAME executePipeline() helper
3. runIntentConsultant() stays as a pre-loop step in the facade, NOT in PipelineLoop
4. StageMetrics tracking can stay in the facade (it's lightweight and used by createStageIssue)
5. The `currentCtx` field is needed by git/issue helpers — keep it

**KEY DESIGN DECISION:** Git/issue helper methods (commitStageArtifacts, postPreviewComment, createStageIssue) remain as private methods on the Orchestrator. They are called via the `onStageComplete` callback that PipelineLoop invokes. This avoids passing the entire Orchestrator to PipelineLoop while keeping git-specific logic out of PipelineLoop.

The `StageMetrics` interface and tracking should stay in the facade — stageMetrics.set() happens in onStageComplete, and createStageIssue reads from it.

For stage metrics timing: Initialize `stageMetrics` for each stage BEFORE calling PipelineLoop. You can subscribe to EventBus `stage:start` event to capture start times, or initialize all stage metrics upfront with a default startTime that gets updated.

ALTERNATIVE (simpler): Wire stageMetrics via EventBus. Subscribe to `stage:start` and `stage:complete` events in the facade before calling PipelineLoop. This way metrics are captured without modifying PipelineLoop.
